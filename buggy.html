Our Story

Founded in 2025, POUR Dining emerged from a unique vision: to create an exceptional dining experience while serving as a living laboratory for web accessibility education. Our name, POUR, represents the four fundamental principles of WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines): Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust.

Located in the heart of New York City, our restaurant offers more than just exquisite cuisine. We provide a hands-on learning environment where diners can experience firsthand the challenges faced by users with disabilities when navigating poorly designed websites.

The POUR Philosophy

Every element of our restaurant experience is intentionally designed to mirror common web accessibility issues. From our intentionally low-contrast menus to our keyboard-trap reservation system, we demonstrate the real-world impact of inaccessible design.

Our mission is to educate while we entertain, creating awareness about the importance of inclusive design in the digital world. Through this unique approach, we hope to inspire the next generation of web developers and designers to prioritize accessibility from the start.

WCAG Principles in Practice

Perceivable

Information and user interface components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive. Our intentionally tiny text and low-contrast elements demonstrate what happens when this principle is violated.

Operable

User interface components and navigation must be operable. Try tabbing through our site to experience the frustration of keyboard traps and missing focus indicators.

Understandable

Information and the operation of user interface must be understandable. Our confusing navigation and missing labels show how poor design affects user comprehension.

Robust

Content must be robust enough to be interpreted by a wide variety of user agents, including assistive technologies. Our site intentionally fails this test to demonstrate the importance of robust design.
